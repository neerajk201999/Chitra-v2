# HyperFrames and motion-design studio process audit

**Evidence cut:** 2026-07-23
**HyperFrames source:** `heygen-com/hyperframes@7a294f19562928036dae20d5e73c2637d1e19060`
**Published package checked:** `hyperframes@0.7.68`

## Claim

Chitra cannot currently create everything HyperFrames can create. No neutral
blind benchmark proves Chitra creates better films. HyperFrames has materially
expanded since the 2026-07-18 audit and now has both broader rendering/media
capability and a serious creative-production workflow.

The useful product thesis remains different: Chitra should combine sufficient
expressive breadth with locked intent, provenance, capability honesty,
evidence-bound revision, and a verifiable release. Those trust features are
real strengths, but they do not compensate for a missing mask, imported
animation, custom 3D scene, player, or narration path when a brief needs one.

## Structural conclusion

Chitra's four main artifacts are not the problem:

1. Intake is the creative brief and source-of-truth lock.
2. Direction is the treatment and governing concept.
3. Storyboard is the beat board and shot design.
4. Score is the executable animation/compositing plan.

The missing bridge is cheap proof of the complete film between Storyboard and
motion production. A high-stakes or multi-shot launch should therefore create a
still-only board Score, inspect every shot, and optionally render a cut animatic
with scratch/final audio before choreography is authored. This uses the existing
schema, compiler, renderer, evidence, and conformance paths; a fifth planning IR
would duplicate truth without evidence of value.

## Honest parity boundary

Chitra is not at HyperFrames capability parity while any important brief can be
blocked by:

- masks/mattes/blend modes and deeper local compositions;
- seekable Lottie/Rive/imported animation;
- arbitrary HTML/runtime adapters or an equivalent safe extension boundary;
- custom glTF/PBR/environment/shadow/post-production scenes;
- narration/TTS and word-synchronized Score audio;
- mature player/Studio and interactive review;
- transparent overlays, broader codecs/HDR/color pipeline;
- scalable/cloud rendering.

Literal API parity is not the goal. User-job parity means a requested launch
film can be completed without a deceptive substitute. Each missing substrate
needs a fixed acceptance film, determinism test, install-cost measurement, and
watched-output evidence before it enters the core.

## What can be better

Chitra can become better at the end-to-end job if it proves, rather than merely
claims:

- fewer discarded animation hours through concept/style-frame/animatic gates;
- fewer fabricated or dropped product claims through typed source trace;
- fewer unnoticed delivery defects through rendered evidence and receipts;
- faster surgical revision through addressable IR and retained accepted/rejected
  outcomes;
- equal or higher blind preference on fixed briefs despite a smaller, safer
  authoring surface.

Those are falsifiable advantages. Until independent results exist, they remain
the product hypothesis.
